# Case 15 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica"
**Promo / product at issue:** Chargeback / reversal of a card transaction on Lemon Cash debit card (VISA *4685) for an online purchase at "Enlargee" / immediates.online / CustomerCare-Vip.com (Nike running shoes).
**Amount in dispute:** ARS 12,924.02 (purchase 6,619.66 + taxes 6,304.36) — 22 November 2023.
**Question:** Must Lemon reverse the card transaction that the user (Florencia Conte) characterises as "no reconocida" but which Lemon characterises as a recognised purchase the user now wants cancelled?

## Options
- Favor User
- Favor Lemon
- Refuse to Arbitrate

## Governing rules
1. Política Section A.1 — Protección del consumidor (financial protection).
2. Política Section A.2 — Información (clear info on chargeback eligibility).
3. Política Section A.3 — Transparencia (limits as offered to the consumer).
4. Política Section A.4 — Buena fe (both sides).
5. Política Section A.5 — In dubio pro consumidor (TIEBREAKER only).
6. Política Section A.7 — No discriminación.
7. Section B — T&Cs / chargeback conditions must comply with Section A.
8. Card-network chargeback framework: "unrecognised / unauthorised transaction" vs. "merchant dispute / buyer's remorse / non-delivery" are distinct reason codes with distinct remedies.

## Burden of proof
User asserts fraud / non-authorised transaction → burden on user to substantiate non-authorisation. Lemon, asserting the chargeback conditions were not met, must show the user's own evidence is inconsistent with "fraud" as defined.

## Application
- **Protección (A.1):** Lemon's chargeback remedy protects against unauthorised use; the user's own evidence shows she entered her own name, address, phone, email and card data into the merchant page and then asked to cancel. Protection of an authorised buyer-remorse claim is outside the chargeback scope.
- **Información (A.2):** Lemon's support team told the user the requirements (card denounce, claim button) and, after review, gave a clear written reason that the case did not meet chargeback conditions, pointing to the T&Cs. Adequate.
- **Transparencia (A.3):** The chargeback policy was applied consistently with how it is offered: unauthorised / fraudulent operations, not commercial disputes with the merchant. User's own messages ("cancel the order", "No Longer Wanted") are a merchant dispute.
- **Buena fe (A.4):** User's filing asserts she never provided card data nor authorised the purchase; her own attached screenshots show she did both. This undermines her good-faith characterisation and supports Lemon's. Lemon acted in good faith.
- **In dubio pro consumidor (A.5):** Tiebreaker only — not engaged because the user's own evidence resolves the factual question against her.
- **Educación (A.6):** Lemon directed the user to pursue the dispute with the merchant — correct channel for a non-delivery / cancellation issue.
- **No discriminación (A.7):** Not implicated.
- **T&C compatibility (Section B):** Limiting chargeback to genuine unauthorised transactions, while directing buyer-merchant disputes back to the merchant, is consistent with Section A.

## Jurisdiction / Morality / Validity
Standard consumer-protection chargeback dispute, within Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica" subcourt. No morality or validity issues. Not a candidate for RtA.

## Schelling-Point check
A reasonable, policy-literate juror reading the user's own screenshots — name, address, phone, email pre-filled in the merchant order, plus an explicit "I want to cancel the order" / "No Longer Wanted" selection — would conclude this is a merchant/delivery dispute, not card fraud. Majority Schelling point clusters on Favor Lemon.

## Bias audit
In dubio pro consumidor pulls toward the user, but the user's own attached evidence directly contradicts her core factual claim of non-authorisation. Discounting the user's narrative against her own documentary exhibits is required, not biased.

## VOTE
**Favor Lemon**

**Confidence:** high

## On-chain justification (2–3 short paragraphs, English)

Under Política Sections A.3 (Transparencia) and A.4 (Buena fe), Lemon's chargeback remedy as offered covers unauthorised or fraudulent card use, not commercial disputes between a buyer and a merchant. The user's own exhibits — the CustomerCare-Vip.com order screen showing her full name, shipping address, phone and email pre-populated on a Nike-shoes order, together with her chat to the merchant stating "I want to cancel the order and get my money back now" and her selection of the "No Longer Wanted" reason code — directly contradict her written assertion that she never provided her card data and never authorised the purchase. The transaction was authorised by her; what she now seeks is cancellation because the merchant has not delivered and will not refund.

Applying A.1 (Protección) and Section B, the chargeback T&Cs are compatible with Section A: they protect consumers from genuine fraud while channelling merchant disputes to the merchant, which is the correct legal route for non-delivery or buyer's-remorse claims. Lemon's support team complied with A.2 (Información) by setting out the required steps (card denounce, claim button) and providing a clear, reasoned rejection citing the T&Cs. A.6 (Educación) is satisfied by directing the user to pursue the merchant.

A.5 (In dubio pro consumidor) is a tiebreaker only and is not engaged here, because the user's own documentary evidence resolves the factual question against her — there is no genuine doubt about authorisation. Vote: Favor Lemon, high confidence.
