# Case 10 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica"
**Promo / product at issue:** "Reintegro Pagos de Servicios" — Lemon promotion (02–13 Oct 2023). TyC document covers both this reintegro and a parallel "Sorteo Pagos de Servicios". User also mentions the lottery chance, but the disputed amount concerns only the missing 4th reintegro.
**Amount in dispute:** 50% reintegro on a 4th utility-bill payment, capped at AR$1,000 (i.e. up to AR$1,000 missing).
**Question:** Did Lemon's in-app promotional banner — which announced 50% reintegro "Por cada factura que pagues con USDC" capped only at "AR$1000 por factura" — bind Lemon to honour a 4th reintegro, given that the 3-invoice ceiling appears only in the linked T&Cs?

## Options
- Favor User
- Favor Lemon
- Refuse to Arbitrate

## Governing rules
1. **Principle 1 — Protección del consumidor.**
2. **Principle 2 — Información clara, veraz y completa** (must reach the consumer in the offer as presented).
3. **Principle 3 — Transparencia** — no misleading advertising; material terms must not be hidden.
4. **Principle 4 — Buena fe** (both sides).
5. **Principle 5 — In dubio pro consumidor** (tiebreaker only).
6. **TyC §1.2 ("Procedimiento para validar la promoción"):** "Tope hasta el equivalente a 1000ARS pesos por factura **y hasta tres facturas por usuarios durante la vigencia de la promoción**."
7. **TyC §1.3:** "Las decisiones de la EMPRESA … serán definitivas e irrecurribles" — catch-all immunity clause invoked impliedly by Lemon.
8. Argentine statutory backdrop referenced by user: Ley 22.802 (Lealtad Comercial) and Ley 24.240 (Defensa del Consumidor) — material promotional conditions must be communicated clearly and prominently.

## Burden of proof
User must show the offer as presented to him contained no quantity cap; Lemon must show that any limit it now invokes was clearly and conspicuously communicated, not buried.

## Application
- **Protección:** The consumer relied on the headline figures in the banner ("Por cada factura... Tope de reintegro: AR$1000 por factura"). The plain reading creates a per-invoice cap, with no quantity cap. Protección favours the consumer who took the issuer at its word.
- **Información (Principle 2):** The offer **as presented** in the app banner omitted a material term — the 3-invoice quantity cap. A reasonable consumer reading "Por cada factura que pagues" with only a per-factura monetary cap will infer no quantity ceiling. Information was incomplete.
- **Transparencia (Principle 3):** Material limits buried in a linked TyC URL, while the banner advertises in language that affirmatively suggests no quantity limit ("Por cada factura... Tope: AR$1000 por factura"), is precisely the misleading-advertising pattern that Section A targets. Lemon's own support agent effectively conceded this ("para ser más claros en futuras promociones").
- **Buena fe:** User acted in good faith: paid 4 bills, then contacted support. Lemon's agent showed good faith too (referred user to Kleros, did not stonewall) but the marketing copy itself is the breach.
- **In dubio:** Not needed — Information and Transparencia decide the case; if it were close, in dubio would push the same way.
- **Educación / No discriminación:** N/A.
- **T&C compatibility (Section B):** TyC §1.2's three-invoice cap is **substantively reasonable** as a business limit — but it is not compatible with Section A *in the way Lemon deployed it*, because that material cap was withheld from the consumer-facing banner. The cap therefore cannot control the dispute when the headline offer omitted it. TyC §1.3's "decisiones definitivas e irrecurribles" catch-all immunity is **not compatible** with Section A and cannot override Principles 2 and 3.

Proper Kleros consumer-protection question; not moral/criminal; case is well-formed; sufficient evidence. No RtA filter triggered.

## Schelling-Point check
A diligent, policy-literate juror reading the banner screenshot first and the TyC second would converge on Favor User: the banner is unambiguous about per-invoice cap only, the omission of a quantity cap is material, and Lemon's support agent effectively admitted the lack of clarity. This is the focal answer under Argentine consumer-protection norms.

## On-chain justification (English)

The dispositive evidence is the in-app banner screenshot, which Lemon does not rebut. It announces a 50% reintegro "Por cada factura que pagues con USDC" with a single express limit — "Tope de reintegro: AR$1000 por factura." A consumer reading that text reasonably concludes there is a monetary cap per invoice but no cap on the number of qualifying invoices. The 3-invoice ceiling exists only in the linked T&Cs (§1.2). Under Principle 2 (Información clara, veraz y completa) and Principle 3 (Transparencia), material limits must reach the consumer through the offer as presented, not be relegated to fine print — the interpretive guidance treats hidden limits as a likely violation.

Lemon's own support agent acknowledged the lack of clarity ("lo tenemos en cuenta para ser más claros en futuras promociones") and directed the user to Kleros — an unrebutted reliance-type admission that strengthens the user's case. Under Section B, the TyC three-invoice clause is substantively lawful as a business term but is not compatible with Section A when deployed to defeat a consumer who never saw it in the offer. The catch-all "decisiones definitivas e irrecurribles" in TyC §1.3 cannot override Principles 1–4.

Remedy: honour the missing fourth reintegro at 50% of that invoice, capped at AR$1,000, consistent with the offer as the consumer reasonably understood it.
