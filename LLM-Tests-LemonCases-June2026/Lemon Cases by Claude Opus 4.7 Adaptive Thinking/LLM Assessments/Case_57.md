# Case 57 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica"
**Promo / product at issue:** Lemon app — USDT (TRC20) outgoing transfers initiated by user to a third-party TRON address (TCwtqXU4TztQo33U7ZF92W2tVphydUWEEv) in connection with an off-platform "investment" proposed by a third party.
**Amount in dispute:** ARS 80,000 (initial "investment") + a further ARS 80,000 demanded by the third party to "recover" the funds. Two on-chain sends shown: ~22.00 USDT and ~36.72 USDT.
**Question:** Is Lemon responsible for refunding the user the value sent off-platform to a third-party Tron address in connection with an external "investment" scheme that the user voluntarily executed?

## Options
- Favor User / Favor Lemon / Refuse to Arbitrate

## Governing rules
1. **Política Sección A — Protección al Consumidor** (priority axis, but conditioned on Lemon's sphere of control).
2. **Información / Transparencia** — Lemon must clearly communicate operational risks and the irreversibility of crypto transactions.
3. **Buena fe** — both parties act in good faith; the user's own statements are admissions.
4. **Educación** — Lemon has a duty to educate users about safe operation (security tips, anti-fraud guidance).
5. **In dubio pro consumidor** — TIEBREAKER only when genuine factual/legal doubt remains after applying the above.
6. **T&Cs** — irreversibility of crypto transactions and Lemon's role as a non-custodial transmission service for user-initiated sends; user retains responsibility for destination address and counterparty.

## Burden of proof
The user, as the party seeking a refund / change to the default state (transactions are complete and irreversible), bears the burden of showing Lemon failed in a duty owed to him. Default state otherwise: completed on-chain transfers stand.

## Application
- **Protección:** Consumer protection is owed within Lemon's sphere of control. The loss arose from a voluntary user-initiated transfer to an external Tron address controlled by a third party — outside Lemon's control. No defect in Lemon's service.
- **Información:** The in-app pre-send screen submitted by the user himself shows the destination, network, fees, total, and an explicit irreversibility warning. Information duty satisfied AS PRESENTED.
- **Transparencia:** No hidden fee, hidden limit, or undisclosed term is at issue. The dispute is about an external scam, not opaque Lemon conduct.
- **Buena fe:** User's own descargo concedes the funds went to a third party promising returns and then demanded a second payment to "recover" the first — a textbook scam pattern. Lemon's response is consistent and non-evasive.
- **In dubio:** Not engaged — there is no residual doubt. Facts are admitted by the claimant.
- **Educación:** Lemon evidences proactive security education (dedicated Instagram "Seguridad" highlight; wiki articles on wallet safety, MFA, online wallets, navigating Web3, avoiding scams). Duty discharged.
- **No discriminación:** Not in issue.
- **T&C compatibility (Sección B):** T&C reliance on irreversibility of crypto transfers and user responsibility for counterparty/destination is fully compatible with Sección A here — invoking it does not override any consumer protection because no Sección A duty was breached.

## Jurisdiction / Morality / Validity
Pass. Correct subcourt (Blockchain No Técnica, Lemon dispute). No General Court morality trigger against Lemon. The bad actor is the off-platform scammer, not a party to this dispute. Question is well-formed and answerable.

## Schelling-Point check
A diligent panel will converge on Favor Lemon. The user openly admits the loss is to a third party scammer; Lemon's UI shows full pre-send disclosure and an irrevocability warning that the user accepted; Lemon evidences ongoing security education. Advance-fee / recovery-room scam losses on user-initiated outbound crypto transfers are not attributable to the on/off-ramp.

## Bias audit
No reliance on party identity, sympathy, or rhetorical polish. The user's loss is genuine and regrettable, but materiality is governed by the policy criteria, not by sympathy. Decision rests on the user's own admissions and the Lemon-side disclosures shown in the user's own screenshots.

## VOTE
**Favor Lemon**

**Confidence:** high

## On-chain justification (English, for on-chain submission)

The claimant's own descargo admits the dispute concerns an external "investment" arranged with a third party, to whom he voluntarily sent USDT via Lemon on the Tron network, and from whom he was then asked for a second deposit to "recover" the first — an advance-fee / recovery-room scam pattern wholly external to Lemon. Under Política de Lemon Sección A, the duty of Protección runs to the consumer within the provider's sphere of control; it does not transform Lemon into an insurer of off-platform counterparties chosen by the user.

The user's own evidence (the "Enviar USDT" confirmation screen for destination TCwtq…EEv on Tron TRC20) demonstrates that Lemon disclosed the destination, network, fees, total, and the explicit irreversibility of crypto transactions before requiring an active "Desliza para confirmar" gesture. The "Retiro Completo" screen confirms the user authorised and executed the transfer. The duties of Información and Transparencia are satisfied AS PRESENTED, and the Buena fe analysis turns on the user's admitted authorisation. Lemon further evidences its Educación duty through dedicated security content on its Instagram and wiki ("Seguridad" highlight, articles on wallet safety, MFA, Web3 navigation).

Because Sección A is not breached, the T&C clauses on irreversibility and user responsibility for destination/counterparty (Sección B) apply normally and are compatible with Sección A in this case. In dubio pro consumidor is a tiebreaker and is not engaged where the claimant has admitted the operative facts. The Schelling answer is Favor Lemon.
