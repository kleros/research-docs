# Case 85 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica" (Lemon Cash subcourt)
**Promo / product at issue:** Automatic migration of Fantom ($FTM) tokens to Sonic ($S) on Lemon Cash, announced for 14/01/2025
**Amount in dispute:** USD 28.2792 (claimed lost opportunity = 91.223257 tokens x (USD 0.84 - USD 0.53))
**Question:** Did Lemon fail to honour its Fantom-to-Sonic migration announcement and is the user entitled to compensation for the resulting unavailability period?

## Options
- Favor User
- Favor Lemon
- Refuse to Arbitrate

## Governing rules
1. **Política Seccion A.2 — Información:** the user must receive truthful, complete and timely information about the product/service as presented.
2. **Política Seccion A.3 — Transparencia:** key terms (dates, restrictions, limits) must be disclosed clearly before the user commits.
3. **Política Seccion A.4 — Buena fe:** both parties must act loyally; the provider cannot rely on reservations that contradict the offer as presented.
4. **Política Seccion A.1 — Protección al usuario:** protective construction in the user's favour for material breaches.
5. **Política Seccion A.5 — In dubio pro consumidor:** TIEBREAKER when, after weighing evidence, the balance is genuinely even.
6. **Política Seccion B — T&C vs Seccion A:** generic catch-all clauses in T&Cs (such as clause 5.7) cannot override the substantive consumer protections of Section A, and cannot retroactively rewrite the offer as presented.
7. **Causation/damages principle (general):** speculative or hypothetical damages (a sale that was never attempted) require some evidentiary support of intent or capacity to realise the gain.

## Burden of proof
The user bears the burden of proving (a) that Lemon breached the offer as presented, and (b) that a quantifiable harm flowed from that breach. Once a clear offer-as-presented breach is shown, the in dubio tiebreaker applies on residual doubts about quantum.

## Application
- **Protección:** the user is a retail consumer who relied on a dated public announcement. Reliance is evidenced by his explicit decision (per chat) not to sell or withdraw before 12/01.
- **Información:** Lemon's offer-as-presented stated automatic conversion on 14/01 with the tokens then "available in S in your app". Conversion was not credited until 20/01. The information about the date was not honoured. This is a clear Información gap.
- **Transparencia:** Lemon did disclose, in the same e-mail, that "some functions like withdrawals and exchange/sale may be limited for a few days." That covers post-conversion functional limits but does NOT cover a 6-day delay in the conversion itself. The conversion date was presented as fixed, not contingent.
- **Buena fe:** Lemon eventually delivered 1:1 and communicated through support. That weighs in Lemon's favour. But invoking T&Cs clause 5.7 (a catch-all internal reservation) to neutralise a specific, public, dated promise is the kind of move Section B is designed to bar.
- **In dubio:** applied only as a tiebreaker. There is genuine residual doubt about quantum: the user never attempted to sell, so the USD 28.28 figure is theoretical. However, the breach (failure to convert on the announced date) is established without doubt. The tiebreaker resolves the quantum doubt in favour of the consumer to the extent the loss is reasonable and proximate.
- **Educación / No discriminación:** not engaged.
- **T&C compatibility:** clause 5.7 governs limits on operations on listed digital assets generally; it does not authorise Lemon to delay a specific, publicly announced migration date. Reading 5.7 to immunise the delay would override Section A and is barred by Section B.

Pass — Blockchain No Técnica is the correct subcourt; no morality issue; question is well-formed and answerable.

## Schelling-Point check
A diligent panel reading Lemon's own e-mail will see two distinct promises: (i) conversion occurs on 14/01, (ii) post-conversion functions may be limited for a few days. Lemon's defence collapses (i) and (ii) into a single "limitation". That is not the obvious reading. The obvious reading is that the conversion date was firm and Lemon missed it by six days. The Información/Transparencia breach is therefore the focal answer. The harder question — how much, if anything, to award — is where reasonable jurors may differ, but the user's amount (USD 28.28) is small, arithmetically transparent, and grounded in Lemon's own price displays.

## On-chain justification (2–3 short paragraphs, English)

Under Section A of the Política, the offer as presented governs Información and Transparencia. Lemon's own 9 January 2025 e-mail told users that, from 14 January, $FTM holdings would be "automatically transformed into $S and you will have them available in S in your app". The same e-mail separately warned that "some functions like withdrawals and exchange/sale may be limited for a few days." Two distinct promises: (i) the conversion itself on 14/01, and (ii) limited but available functions afterward. Lemon's own support chat and emails confirm the conversion was only credited on 20 January — six days late. That is a breach of promise (i), not merely an instance of promise (ii). Reliance is documented: the user explicitly chose not to sell or withdraw before 12/01 because he expected the 14/01 conversion.

Lemon's defence relies on T&Cs clause 5.7, which permits restrictions on operations for broad operational, commercial, or strategic reasons. Section B of the Política does not allow a generic catch-all in the T&Cs to override the specific, dated, publicly announced offer. Clause 5.7 may justify post-migration functional limits (which were disclosed), but it does not authorise unilaterally postponing a specific announced migration date. The Información and Transparencia breach therefore stands.

On quantum, the user's USD 28.28 figure is theoretical: he did not attempt to sell at the peak, so the loss is an unrealised opportunity rather than a proven realised loss. However, the figure is small, arithmetically transparent, grounded in Lemon's own in-app price displays, and the residual doubt about realisation intent is resolved by Principle A.5 (in dubio pro consumidor) once the underlying breach is established. I vote Favor User.
