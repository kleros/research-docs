# Case 61 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica"
**Promo / product at issue:** "Comprá $ETH y ganá más $ETH" — ETH Week promotion (Lemon, Argentina). Buy >= 20.000 ARS in ETH, receive 2.000 ARS in ETH as a gift, cap 2.000 ARS.
**Amount in dispute:** User alleges a 5.000 ARS gift was owed (not received). Actual promo gift = 2.000 ARS.
**Question:** Did Lemon owe the user a 5.000 ARS ETH gift under the promotion, and did it fail to deliver the promised reward?

## Options
- Favor User / Favor Lemon / Refuse to Arbitrate

## Governing rules
1. **Política Sección A.2 — Información:** the offer as presented to the consumer governs; the promotional terms shown in the email define entitlement.
2. **Política Sección A.3 — Transparencia:** terms must be clear and not hidden — here, terms were stated openly in the same email body.
3. **Política Sección A.4 — Buena fe:** both parties must act honestly in describing the offer and their performance.
4. **Política Sección A.1 — Protección:** consumer protection where there is genuine asymmetry or hidden term.
5. **Política Sección A.5 — In dubio pro usuario:** tiebreaker only when genuine doubt remains.
6. **Offer-as-presented doctrine (guidance):** clear, visible limits in the offer are enforceable; hidden limits would be a violation — none hidden here.

## Burden of proof
The user, as claimant seeking a change from the default state (Lemon performed), bears the burden of showing (a) the promised benefit was 5.000 ARS and (b) that nothing was credited. Lemon need only show the actual promo terms and that it credited what those terms required.

## Application
- **Protección:** No hidden term or asymmetry; the 2.000 ARS cap was stated in the same email body, in the same bullet list, in plain Spanish. Consumer-protection concern not triggered.
- **Información:** The offer "as presented" to the user explicitly says "$2.000 más" and "máximo 2000 ARS en $ETH de regalo". This governs. The 5.000 ARS figure appears nowhere in the offer.
- **Transparencia:** Terms were transparent — bullet points 1, 2, 3 plainly visible, no fine print, no hidden cap.
- **Buena fe:** Lemon acted in good faith — described the promo accurately and credited the 2.000-ARS-equivalent in ETH on 1/8/2024. The user's claim of a 5.000 ARS entitlement is unsupported by any document and contradicted by the user's own submitted evidence.
- **In dubio pro usuario:** Not engaged — there is no genuine doubt. The promo text is unambiguous and the credit is documented.
- **Educación:** Not in dispute.
- **No discriminación:** Not in dispute.
- **T&C compatibility:** No T&C catch-all is being relied upon to override Section A; the offer-as-presented itself defines the entitlement.

## Jurisdiction / Morality / Validity
Pass. Correct subcourt (Blockchain No Técnica, Argentine consumer promo). No morality issue. Question is well-formed and answerable from the record.

## Schelling-Point check
A diligent panel reading the same email screenshot (submitted by both sides) sees "$2.000" three times and "5.000" nowhere. The literal, obvious reading is that the promo's cap is 2.000 ARS and that Lemon credited it. Convergence on Favor Lemon is the focal answer.

## On-chain justification (2–3 short paragraphs, English)
Under Política Sección A.2 (Información) and A.3 (Transparencia), the offer as presented to the consumer governs entitlement. The promotional email — submitted by both parties and therefore uncontested — states three times that the gift is "$2.000" and that "vas recibir como máximo 2000 ARS en $ETH de regalo". The 5.000 ARS figure on which the user's claim rests appears nowhere in the offer. The terms were stated plainly in the body bullets, with no hidden limit, so the offer-as-presented doctrine fully favors Lemon and the consumer-protection clause (A.1) is not triggered.

On performance, Lemon produced an internal admin-panel record showing a MANUAL_DEPOSIT of 0.00044813 ETH credited on 1/8/2024 15:50:42 to user ID 1738460 (matching the case ID), Lemontag "gustavofibanez", with the concept "Acreditación premio ETH Week". The user did not rebut this record; under standard Kleros practice, unrebutted documentary evidence is presumed genuine. Lemon therefore performed the promotion as presented.

In dubio pro usuario (A.5) is a tiebreaker only, and there is no genuine doubt here — the user's own evidence defeats the 5.000 ARS claim, and Lemon has documented timely performance of the 2.000-ARS-equivalent ETH credit. Vote: Favor Lemon.
