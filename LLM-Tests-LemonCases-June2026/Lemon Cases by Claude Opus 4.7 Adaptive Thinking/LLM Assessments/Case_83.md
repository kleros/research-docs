# Case 83 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica" (Lemon Cash arbitration policy, Argentina)
**Promo / product at issue:** Crypto recovery service for deposits sent through unsupported networks (FAQ article + T&Cs cl. 13.6 / 13.7).
**Amount in dispute:** Not stated. User sent USDT on the OPBNB network (minimum recoverable per FAQ for "otras redes" = USD 1).
**Question:** Must Lemon recover the USDT-OPBNB deposit (charging network costs + 10% commission) as described in its public FAQ, or is the recovery lawfully refused because OPBNB is not a supported network under cl. 13.7?

## Options
- **Favor User** — Lemon ordered to attempt recovery per the FAQ, deducting network costs and the 10% commission.
- **Favor Lemon** — Refusal upheld; OPBNB is unsupported and recovery is discretionary, not guaranteed.
- **Refuse to Arbitrate** — Only if jurisdiction / morality / validity fails.

## Governing rules
1. **Section A — Política del Consumidor** (overrides T&Cs when in conflict): Protección, Información, Transparencia, Buena fe, In dubio pro consumidor (tiebreaker), Educación, No discriminación.
2. **Lemon FAQ (offer as presented):** "Si podemos recuperarlos…" — recovery is expressly **conditional** ("Si podemos…"), requires the user to contact support with the hash, is subject to review, and only then triggers the USD 1 minimum + network costs + 10% commission. The FAQ does **not** promise universal recovery from every chain.
3. **T&Cs cl. 13.7** ("Activos Digitales No Listados"): Assets sent through unlisted networks "no podrán verse reflejados en su cuenta" and Lemon "no se encuentra obligada a permitir la operación de Activos Digitales que no estén listados."
4. **General Court morality clause:** No party has engaged in murder/slavery/rape/violence/theft/perjury — passes.

## Burden of proof
On the User, as the party seeking a change from the default state (funds not credited). User must prove either (a) Lemon promised unconditional recovery for OPBNB-USDT, or (b) Lemon's refusal violates a Section A principle that overrides cl. 13.7.

## Application
- **Protección al Consumidor:** Consumer interests are protected when policies are clear and applied consistently. The FAQ itself flags conditionality ("Si podemos…", "revisamos si puede recuperarse"). Refusing a recovery the operator deems technically/commercially unfeasible is not, by itself, a violation of Protección.
- **Información:** The FAQ provides the conditions ("Si podemos", min USD 1 for "otras redes", 10% commission). It also expressly tells users supported networks are listed in the in-app UI and a separate help-centre article. Information is adequate, even if not exhaustive.
- **Transparencia:** The "offer as presented" in the FAQ is **explicitly conditional**, not a blanket guarantee. A reasonable reader sees "Si podemos recuperarlos" as the operative qualifier. No hidden limit is being invoked — cl. 13.7 simply makes the same conditionality binding. No transparencia violation.
- **Buena fe:** Lemon engaged with the user via support and explained the limitation. No bad-faith conduct shown.
- **In dubio pro consumidor:** Tiebreaker only. The FAQ's conditional wording is **not** genuinely ambiguous — "Si podemos recuperarlos" plainly conditions the offer on Lemon's technical ability to recover. No tie to break.
- **Educación:** Lemon points to its Wiki + help-centre articles. Adequate.
- **No discriminación:** Not engaged.
- **T&C compatibility with Section A:** Cl. 13.7 disclaims an automatic obligation to recover funds from unlisted networks. This is consistent with the FAQ's conditional phrasing, so cl. 13.7 does not contradict Section A on these facts. The T&C is not being used as a catch-all to override an unconditional public promise — because no unconditional public promise was made.

## On-chain justification

The User's claim rests on the Lemon FAQ that describes recovery of crypto sent through unsupported networks at "USD 1 minimum, network costs, and 10% commission." Read as presented, that FAQ is **explicitly conditional**: "Si podemos recuperarlos…" and "contactá a soporte por el chat de la app con el hash y revisamos si puede recuperarse." The FAQ does not promise universal recovery from every chain; it announces a discretionary review subject to technical feasibility, and it expressly cross-refers to T&Cs cl. 13.6 / 13.7 and to a separate help-centre article listing supported deposit networks. There is no "hidden limit": the conditionality is on the face of the offer the User relied on.

Applying Section A: **Información** and **Transparencia** are satisfied because the conditional wording is plain, the supported-networks list is accessible from the same article, and cl. 13.7 mirrors (rather than contradicts) the FAQ. **Protección al Consumidor** is not breached by a good-faith refusal to perform a technically unsupported recovery. The User invokes the **In dubio** tiebreaker, but it applies only to a genuine ambiguity; "Si podemos recuperarlos" is not ambiguous on these facts. **Buena fe** is preserved — Lemon's support, on the User's own account, engaged with the request and explained that OPBNB is not operated.

The unrebutted evidence (both parties submitted the same FAQ screenshot, and Lemon quoted cl. 13.7 verbatim) supports Lemon. The User identifies no specific misrepresentation, no support-chat confirmation that recovery would occur, and no concrete reliance beyond the (correctly conditional) FAQ text. The burden of proof lies on the User and is not discharged. Vote: Favor Lemon.
