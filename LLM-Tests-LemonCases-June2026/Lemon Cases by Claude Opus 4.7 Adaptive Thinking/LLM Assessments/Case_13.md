# Case 13 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica"
**Promo / product at issue:** "Reintegro Pagos de Servicios" — 50% reintegro on first 3 service-payment invoices paid with USDC, capped at AR$1,000 per invoice, vigencia 02/10/2023–13/10/2023 (Lanin Pay S.A. de C.V. / Lemon Cash).
**Amount in dispute:** User claims approximately $9,000 (pesos) reintegro; Lemon says cap is AR$1,000 per invoice.
**Question:** Did Lemon breach the duty of clear/precise information by offering a 50% reintegro on service payments without adequately disclosing that the benefit required payment in USDC and was capped at AR$1,000 per invoice?

## Options
- Favor User
- Favor Lemon
- Refuse to Arbitrate

## Governing rules
1. **Política Section A** — 1. Protección, 2. Información, 3. Transparencia, 4. Buena fe, 5. In dubio pro consumidor (tiebreaker only), 6. Educación, 7. No discriminación.
2. **Argentine Consumer Protection** — Ley 24.240 arts. 4 (información), 7–8 (oferta y publicidad vinculante); CCyC arts. 1100, 1103.
3. **Promotion T&Cs (Lanin Pay 27-Sep-2023)** — clause 1 requires payment with $USDC via "Pagos de Servicios"; clause 2 caps reintegro at "1000ARS pesos por factura y hasta tres facturas por usuario"; acreditación within 5 días hábiles post-promo.
4. **Section B compatibility check:** the USDC requirement and AR$1,000/3-invoice cap are also disclosed in the in-app promotional pop-up itself, so the T&C limits are NOT hidden — they are compatible with Section A principles 2 & 3 (information and transparency).

## Burden of proof
User must show the offer, AS PRESENTED to a reasonable consumer, was misleading or omitted material conditions. Lemon, having invoked T&C limits, must show those limits were communicated up-front in the offer (not buried in linked T&Cs).

## Application
- **Protección (1):** Consumer is protected against misleading offers. Here the in-app pop-up itself names both load-bearing limits (USDC + AR$1,000 cap), so protection does not require rewriting the offer.
- **Información (2):** The promo screen states the USDC condition and the AR$1,000-per-factura cap in plain text on the same pop-up that triggers the action. This is "información cierta, clara y detallada" at the point of decision — not buried in linked T&Cs.
- **Transparencia (3):** Both load-bearing limits (currency requirement and per-invoice cap) are visible in the offer AS PRESENTED. Transparencia is satisfied.
- **Buena fe (4):** Support agent Vero gave honest, generic timing information; she did not represent that the user would receive a reintegro in pesos or that USDC was not required. No bad faith.
- **In dubio (5):** Not invoked — the evidence is one-sided. Lemon's promo screenshot resolves the factual question (offer disclosed USDC + cap).
- **Educación / No discriminación (6, 7):** N/A.
- **T&C compatibility (Section B):** The clauses Lemon invokes (USDC requirement + AR$1,000 cap + 3-invoice limit) are also disclosed in the offer itself, so they are not hidden surprise terms. They are compatible with Section A principles 2 and 3. The clause 6 "exclusión de responsabilidad" in the Sorteo section is a catch-all immunity clause and would NOT control under Section B, but Lemon does not need to rely on it here.

## Jurisdiction / Morality / Validity
Properly framed B2C consumer dispute. No morality, jurisdictional, or malformation issues. RtA not warranted.

## Schelling-Point check
A diligent juror reading the case will see Lemon's own evidence (the promo pop-up) plainly states "pagues con USDC" and "Tope de reintegro: AR$1000 por factura". User does not allege he paid in USDC; he argues he should not have had to. That is a legal argument against the promo's structure, not evidence of misleading information. Most diligent jurors would converge on Favor Lemon.

## Bias audit
Sympathy pull toward an Argentine consumer claiming a Spanish-language fintech misled him is real, and the support-chat tone ("Correcto", "Sí, Fede") does invite a reliance reading. Resisting: Vero never confirms qualification, only timing; and the very screenshot Lemon produces in its defence rebuts the user's "falta de información precisa" thesis on its face. Also resisting reverse bias against the user: the claimed amount ($9,000) is internally inconsistent with even the most user-favourable reading of the promo (max AR$3,000), which weakens credibility.

## VOTE
**Favor Lemon**

**Confidence:** high

## On-chain justification (2–3 short paragraphs, English)

Under Política Section A principles 2 (Información) and 3 (Transparencia), the test is what the offer disclosed AS PRESENTED to the consumer at the point of decision. Lemon's own evidence — the in-app promotional pop-up — states on its face: "te reintegramos el 50% de las 3 primeras facturas que pagues con USDC hasta el 13/10" and "Tope de reintegro: AR$1000 por factura". Both load-bearing limits (the USDC payment requirement and the per-invoice cap) are visible in the offer itself, not buried in linked T&Cs. The user's argument that the conditions were learned only after contracting is contradicted by the promo screen Lemon produced and which the user did not rebut.

The user's appeal to CCyC art. 765 (peso of legal tender) is misplaced. Lemon did not refuse to accept pesos for the underlying service payment; it offered a *promotional* reintegro conditioned on USDC use. A bonus tied to a specific payment instrument is lawful and does not displace legal-tender rules. The support-chat with agent "Vero" only confirms the generic 5-business-day credit window post-promo; Vero never represents that the user qualified or that the USDC condition was waived, so principle 4 (Buena fe) is not engaged in the user's favour. Principle 5 (in dubio pro consumidor) cannot be invoked because there is no genuine doubt on the record.

Finally, the user's $9,000 claim is internally inconsistent with the promo's maximum possible benefit (AR$1,000 × 3 invoices = AR$3,000) even on the most user-favourable reading, further undermining the merits. Lemon's T&C clauses are compatible with Section A under the Section B check because the same limits appear in the in-app offer. Vote: Favor Lemon, high confidence.
