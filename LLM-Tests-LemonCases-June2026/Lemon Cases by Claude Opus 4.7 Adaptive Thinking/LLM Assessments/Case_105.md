# Case 105 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica"
**Promo / product at issue:** P2P transfer via Mercado Pago to a third-party Lemon user's CVU, intended by the sender to fund a 1xbet account; funds credited by 1xbet as a non-withdrawable "bono".
**Amount in dispute:** ARS 28,000
**Question:** Is Lemon liable to refund a transfer that entered the Lemon system as an inbound CVU receipt to an individual user's account, where the loss arose from the destination 1xbet platform crediting the funds as a bono?

## Options
- Favor User / Favor Lemon / Refuse to Arbitrate

## Governing rules
1. Política Section A — Protección, Información, Transparencia, Buena fe, In dubio (tiebreaker), Educación, No discriminación.
2. The offer-as-presented governs Información and Transparencia (Lemon must be held to what it actually advertised/sold to this user).
3. Catch-all immunity in Lemon T&Cs cannot override Section A, but Section A only attaches where Lemon is the relevant counterparty to the consumer transaction at issue.
4. Unrebutted evidence is presumed genuine; reliance on support-chat confirmations creates a Transparencia hook only against the party that issued them.
5. Burden of proof on the party seeking change from the default state (User must show Lemon breached a duty owed to him).

## Burden of proof
On the User to show that Lemon (not 1xbet, not the individual CVU holder) owed him a duty that was breached.

## Application
- **Protección al Consumidor:** The consumer-merchant relationship for the bono dispute is User↔1xbet (and arguably User↔CVU-holder Sabrina Del Valle Rojas). Lemon's role was passive CVU receipt for one of its retail users. Protección applies, but Lemon's protective duty is limited to acting on reports against accounts in its system; once reported, Lemon suspended the account.
- **Información / Transparencia (offer-as-presented):** No Lemon offer is in the record. The user did not buy a 1xbet top-up from Lemon, did not interact with any Lemon promo, and was never told by Lemon that the funds would be usable on 1xbet. Lemon never made a representation that 1xbet would treat the funds as non-bono. No hidden limits in any Lemon offer are implicated.
- **Buena fe:** Lemon's documented conduct (preventive block on the receiving account once notified, written explanation, offer to cooperate with police investigation) is consistent with good-faith handling. The user's 7-day delay between transfer (06-May) and report (13-May) materially reduced any chance of asset recovery — fondos retirados a los minutos. This is corroborated by the "Seen • 8d" timestamp on Pablo's email.
- **In dubio:** Tiebreaker only — facts here are not genuinely tied; the structure of the transaction is clear from the MP comprobante.
- **Educación:** Not directly engaged.
- **No discriminación:** Not engaged.
- **T&C compatibility:** Lemon's defence does not rest on a catch-all exclusion; it rests on the absence of any contractual or factual link between Lemon and the bono outcome. Section A is therefore not "overridden" — it is simply not breached on these facts.

## Jurisdiction / Morality / Validity
Pass. Standard Lemon consumer dispute in the correct subcourt. Gambling-funding context does not trigger the General Court morality clause (no murder/slavery/rape/violence/theft/perjury enumerated conduct).

## Schelling-Point check
A diligent panel will read the MP comprobante and see the receiver is an individual Lemon user (Sabrina Del Valle Rojas), not Lemon-as-merchant. The bono classification was made by 1xbet downstream. Lemon acted on the report it received. The literal, obvious reading is that Lemon owed no duty to guarantee a specific treatment of funds by 1xbet, and the realistic Schelling answer is Favor Lemon.

## Bias audit
Sympathy for a consumer who lost ARS 28,000 to what looks like an informal P2P "cambista" funding 1xbet is real but immaterial to whether Lemon breached Section A. Removing that sympathy and re-running the analysis yields the same outcome. No reliance on party identity, follower count, or rhetorical style.

## VOTE
**Favor Lemon**

**Confidence:** high

## On-chain justification (2–3 short paragraphs, English)
The user's own primary evidence — the Mercado Pago comprobante of 06-May-2025 14:08 hs — shows the transfer left Mercado Pago and entered Lemon as an inbound CVU credit to an individual Lemon account-holder (Sabrina Del Valle Rojas, CUIT 27-43203269-3). Lemon was not the merchant, did not run any promo connected to this payment, and did not advertise any 1xbet outcome to the user. The loss the user complains of — funds being credited as a non-withdrawable "bono" on 1xbet — was produced downstream by 1xbet, not by Lemon. Under Política Section A, the duties of Información, Transparencia and Protección attach to the offer Lemon actually presented; here there is no such offer in the record, so no clause of Section A is engaged against Lemon.

On Buena fe, Lemon's documented response is consistent with diligent handling: once the user reported the situation, Lemon suspended the receiving account and provided a written explanation directing the user to file a police report so the funds in the hands of the recipient could be pursued through criminal channels. Against this, the user waited seven days to report (Pablo's email is marked "Seen • 8d"; transfer was 06-May, contact 13-May). The recipient withdrew the funds within minutes of receipt — that is the moment at which recoverability was lost, and that loss is causally tied to the user's reporting delay, not to any act or omission by Lemon.

In dubio pro consumidor is a tiebreaker and does not apply, because the factual structure is clear from the MP comprobante itself. The dispute the user describes is between him, the individual CVU holder, and 1xbet; Lemon's only role was passive CVU receipt for one of its retail users, and that role does not generate a refund obligation on these facts. Vote: Favor Lemon.
