# Case 64 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica"
**Promo / product at issue:** Crypto deposit recovery — user sent WETH (an unlisted token) on Optimism to a Lemon-associated address
**Amount in dispute:** Not stated; user describes only the WETH transfer (gas fees shown on Optimism Etherscan; the token amount itself is not specified)
**Question:** Must Lemon credit or recover funds when a user sends an unlisted token (WETH on Optimism) to a deposit address, given Lemon's T&C 13.7 and its published list of supported assets?

## Options
- Favor User
- Favor Lemon
- Refuse to Arbitrate

## Governing rules
1. Política de Defensa del Consumidor — Section A: Protección, Información, Transparencia, Buena fe, In dubio pro consumidor (tiebreaker), Educación, No discriminación.
2. Section B: Lemon T&Cs apply only where compatible with Section A.
3. Lemon T&C clause 13.7 — Unlisted Digital Assets: assets sent in error that are not listed on the Platform or via an unlisted network cannot be reflected in the user's account; Lemon is not obliged to operate non-listed assets.
4. Offer-as-presented: the published list of supported tokens (Novedades screen) governs what users may reasonably expect to deposit.

## Burden of proof
User must establish, on a preponderance of evidence, that Lemon failed a duty (Protección/Información/Transparencia/Buena fe). Where evidence is genuinely balanced, in dubio favours the user — but only as a tiebreaker, not a substitute for proof.

## Application
- **Protección:** Lemon's published asset list and T&C 13.7 protected the user ex ante by warning that unlisted tokens cannot be credited. The loss arose from a user-side selection error (WETH vs ETH), not from a platform failure.
- **Información:** The Novedades screen and help-centre articles disclose, in-app, exactly which tokens/networks are supported. WETH is not on the list. The information duty is met.
- **Transparencia:** T&C 13.7 is explicit, plain-language, and uploaded as evidence. Support also explained the issue clearly to the user. Transparency is met.
- **Buena fe:** Support engaged the user, explained the limitation, and shared educational links to prevent recurrence. No bad-faith conduct shown.
- **In dubio pro consumidor:** No genuine evidentiary tie. The user has not contradicted that the asset was WETH, nor shown that Lemon ever offered WETH custody. Tiebreaker does not engage.
- **Educación:** Lemon points to its educational hub and help-centre articles. Educational duty is met.
- **No discriminación:** Not in issue.
- **T&C compatibility:** Clause 13.7 is compatible with Section A — it codifies the natural scope of the service (only listed assets) rather than functioning as a catch-all immunity for platform errors. Operating an unlisted token would require Lemon to custody an asset it does not technically support.

## Jurisdiction / Morality / Validity
Standard consumer-arbitration matter; no illegality, immorality, or jurisdictional defect. Question is decidable. RtA not warranted.

## Schelling-Point check
A diligent juror reading the policy would land on Favor Lemon: the user sent a non-listed token; the offer as presented never included WETH; T&C 13.7 mirrors that limitation; and Lemon's evidence is unrebutted. This is the convergent answer.

## Bias audit
Sympathy for a user who has lost crypto is natural, but the record shows a self-inflicted asset/network selection error against a clearly published supported-asset list. No pro-platform bias either — Lemon's clause is narrow and substantively justified, not a catch-all.

## VOTE
**Favor Lemon**

**Confidence:** high

## On-chain justification (English)
The user's own descargo concedes a transfer was made but offers only a gas-detail Etherscan screenshot that does not identify the asset, destination, or amount. Lemon's evidence — uncontested — establishes that the asset was WETH on Optimism, a token absent from Lemon's published Novedades list of supported assets, and that T&C 13.7 expressly states unlisted assets cannot be reflected in user accounts. The offer as presented to consumers never included WETH custody, so Información and Transparencia were satisfied before the user transacted.

Under Section A, Protección does not require a platform to credit assets it does not technically support and has clearly excluded in advance. Buena fe is satisfied: support engaged the user, explained the technical impossibility, and proactively shared educational resources. Educación is satisfied by Lemon's in-app learning hub and help-centre articles linked in the support chat. No genuine evidentiary tie exists, so in dubio pro consumidor does not engage as a tiebreaker.

Clause 13.7 is compatible with Section A because it defines the scope of the service (listed assets only) rather than operating as a catch-all immunity for platform-side failures. The loss flows from a user-side selection of an unlisted token, not from any breach of the consumer-protection duties. The Schelling answer for a diligent, policy-literate juror is Favor Lemon.
