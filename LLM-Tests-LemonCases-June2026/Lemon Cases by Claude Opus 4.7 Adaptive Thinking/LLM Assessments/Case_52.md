# Case 52 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica"
**Promo / product at issue:** Erroneous third-party transfer into a Lemon account (no Lemon promotion involved)
**Amount in dispute:** ARS $10,000
**Question:** Must Lemon reimburse the user for funds the user mistakenly transferred from Mercado Pago to a different individual's Lemon account?

## Options
- Favor User / Favor Lemon / Refuse to Arbitrate

## Governing rules
1. **Política Section A.1 Protección** — Lemon must protect user assets within its control. It does not extend to seizing other customers' lawful balances.
2. **Política Section A.2 Información / A.3 Transparencia** — Lemon must clearly inform users about how transfers work and the limits of its intervention.
3. **Política Section A.4 Buena fe** — Lemon must act in good faith to assist where it reasonably can.
4. **Política Section A.5 In dubio pro consumidor** — tiebreaker only.
5. **Section B** — T&Cs cannot override Section A; but the absence of any T&C override here is not in itself a violation.
6. **General principle of irreversibility of CVU transfers** under Argentine BCRA/CNV rules: once credited to a third-party account, the receiving institution cannot debit the recipient without consent or a court order. This is a regulatory constraint, not a Lemon discretion.

## Burden of proof
The user is the moving party and must show that Lemon (a) caused the loss, (b) had power to reverse it, or (c) breached a duty owed under Política Section A. Default outcome absent such proof: no reimbursement obligation.

## Application
- **Protección (A.1):** Lemon's protection duty runs to its account holders' lawful balances. The recipient Lencina is a Lemon user whose balance is also protected. Forcing a debit without consent would itself violate A.1 against her. No breach by Lemon.
- **Información (A.2):** Lemon clearly informed the user via support that it cannot unilaterally debit a third party. No breach.
- **Transparencia (A.3):** Support email is explicit about Lemon's role and limits. No breach.
- **Buena fe (A.4):** Evidence shows Lemon contacted the recipient and reported the outcome. That is the reasonable good-faith step. No breach.
- **In dubio (A.5):** Not engaged — there is no genuine doubt; the cause of loss is a user-side input error on Mercado Pago's side, and the funds are lawfully held by an unrelated third party.
- **Educación (A.6) / No discriminación (A.7):** Not engaged.
- **T&C compatibility (Section B):** Lemon does not invoke a T&C carve-out adverse to Section A; it invokes a regulatory limit and the absence of consent. Compatible.

## Jurisdiction / Morality / Validity
Pass. Correct subcourt; no immoral conduct alleged against Lemon; question is well-formed.

## Schelling-Point check
A diligent panel will recognise this as a classic "wrong-account transfer" case where the receiving institution is not liable for the sender's error. Convergence on Favor Lemon is the obvious literal-policy outcome.

## Bias audit
Sympathy for a user out $10,000 is natural but immaterial. The user did not transact with Lemon's product; Lemon is third-party to the underlying Mercado Pago instruction. Removing sympathy does not alter the conclusion.

## VOTE
**Favor Lemon**

**Confidence:** high

## On-chain justification (2–3 short paragraphs, English)
The user mistakenly initiated a Mercado Pago CVU transfer of ARS $10,000 to a third party (Gisela Lencina) who happens to hold a Lemon account. The error originated in the user's own input on Mercado Pago; Lemon was merely the destination institution. Under Política Section A.1 (Protección), Lemon's duty of protection extends to every account holder's lawful balance — including the recipient's — so it cannot debit her funds without her consent or a court order. Argentine CVU transfer rules reinforce this: receiving institutions have no unilateral reversal power.

Lemon discharged its Section A duties of Información, Transparencia and Buena fe by contacting the recipient and explaining the legal limits to the user in writing (support email of 19-Jul-2024, unrebutted). No clause of Section A imposes a reimbursement obligation on a destination wallet for sender-side input errors, and Section B is not engaged because Lemon invokes no adverse T&C — only a regulatory and consensual constraint. The In dubio tiebreaker (A.5) does not activate where the policy reading is clear.

The Schelling-coherent reading is that Lemon is not the cause of the loss and has no power, contractual or regulatory, to reverse it. The user's remedy lies against the recipient (civil claim) or through Mercado Pago's dispute channels, not against Lemon. Vote: Favor Lemon.
