# Case 47 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica"
**Promo / product at issue:** Voluntary peer-to-peer transfer (Mercado Pago to Lemon CVU) for a clothing purchase from a third-party WhatsApp seller ("INDUMENTARIA_FAC...") who failed to deliver.
**Amount in dispute:** ARS $49,700 (single transfer dated 06/07/2024, 11:00 hs).
**Question:** Is Lemon liable to reimburse a user who voluntarily transferred funds, via Mercado Pago, to a Lemon-account holder (Braian Daniel Martinez, CUIT 20-38832689-2) who then allegedly defrauded the user by not delivering merchandise?

## Options
- Favor User
- Favor Lemon
- Refuse to Arbitrate

## Governing rules
1. Política de Protección al Consumidor — Sección A (1 Protección, 2 Información, 3 Transparencia, 4 Buena fe, 5 In dubio pro consumidor (tiebreaker), 6 Educación, 7 No discriminación).
2. Sección B — T&Cs are subordinate to Sección A.
3. Schelling-point reasoning: vote what a diligent, policy-literate juror would converge on.
4. Scope of Lemon's duty: Lemon is a payments/custody platform. Its consumer-protection duties run to its own services (custody, information about its product, execution of user instructions, KYC of its account-holders). It is not an underwriter of off-platform commercial agreements between its users and third-party merchants reached over WhatsApp.
5. Voluntary, authenticated user-instructed transfers, once executed correctly, do not by themselves trigger a refund duty in the absence of a Lemon-side defect (mis-execution, custody failure, breach of own promise, failure of KYC/AML obligations the user can point to as causally relevant).

## Burden of proof
The user — as claimant invoking Lemon's consumer-protection duties — bears the initial burden of showing a Lemon-side breach (defective service, false/missing information, bad-faith conduct, etc.). Lemon then bears the burden of rebutting any prima facie violation. Here the user's own evidence forecloses the prima facie step.

## Application
- **Protección:** The protection duty covers Lemon's own service. There is no allegation that Lemon failed to safeguard the user's funds in custody, mis-routed the transfer, or refused to act. The loss arose from an off-platform commercial agreement (clothing purchase via WhatsApp) with a non-Lemon counterparty. No Protección breach by Lemon.
- **Información:** No specific Lemon-published representation about this transaction has been challenged. The user does not point to any misleading Lemon message that induced the transfer. No Información breach.
- **Transparencia:** The Lemon "Detalle de operación" mirrors the Mercado Pago receipt exactly — same operation number, same CVU, same name, same amount. The execution was transparently displayed to the user. No Transparencia breach.
- **Buena fe:** Lemon executed the user's instruction in good faith. The user does not allege Lemon refused complaints, blocked recovery options, or behaved abusively. The descargo is measured and on point. No Buena fe breach.
- **In dubio pro consumidor:** Tiebreaker only. There is no genuine doubt to break — the user's own evidence shows a voluntary transfer to a third-party scammer, not a Lemon defect. In dubio does not transform third-party fraud into platform liability.
- **Educación:** Lemon publishes safety guidance on official channels (Instagram, app). No evidence of educational failure. Even on a strict reading, there is no causal link between an alleged education gap and this off-platform seller scam.
- **No discriminación:** Not implicated.
- **T&C compatibility (Sección B):** No T&C clause is being invoked to override Sección A. Lemon's defence rests on the absence of a Sección-A duty in respect of third-party commercial fraud, not on a catch-all immunity clause.

## Jurisdiction / Morality / Validity
The dispute is properly framed and within the subcourt. The user's harm is real and the underlying conduct (the seller's fraud) is reprehensible, but the only party before this panel is Lemon, and the policy-relevant question is Lemon's conduct. The proper forum to pursue Braian Daniel Martinez is criminal complaint and civil action — Lemon would be expected to cooperate with judicial information requests, but that is outside the scope of this Kleros vote.

## Schelling-Point check
A diligent, policy-literate juror reading this record converges on Favor Lemon. The user explicitly states the harm came from "una persona que utiliza la plataforma Lemon" — i.e., a third-party account-holder, not Lemon. The user's own receipts show a textbook voluntary push payment. No Sección-A duty of Lemon has been identified, let alone breached. Voting Favor User here would require holding payment platforms strictly liable for every off-platform scam routed through their rails — a result no rational juror cohort would converge on.

## Bias audit
Sympathy for a scam victim is strong and must be acknowledged. I have separated sympathy from liability: the seller is plainly culpable; Lemon, on this record, is not. I have not relied on any T&C immunity clause; I have applied Sección A directly and found no Lemon-side breach. I have given the user the benefit of unrebutted authenticity on all screenshots, which only confirms the voluntary-transfer narrative.

## VOTE
**Favor Lemon**

**Confidence:** high

## On-chain justification (2–3 short paragraphs, English)

The user's own evidence is dispositive. The WhatsApp screenshots show an off-platform commercial agreement with a third-party seller ("INDUMENTARIA_FAC...") for clothing at ARS $49,700. The Mercado Pago "Comprobante de transferencia" 81913237607 and the mirrored Lemon "Detalle de operación" together prove that the user voluntarily instructed a transfer to the CVU, alias and name dictated by the seller, and that Lemon executed that instruction exactly. The harm the user describes — non-delivery of merchandise and the seller going silent — is third-party fraud committed via Lemon rails, not a defect in Lemon's service.

Applying Sección A of the Política de Protección al Consumidor on the merits (not via any T&C catch-all): Protección, Información, Transparencia, Buena fe and Educación all run to Lemon's own conduct, and the record shows no breach on any of them. The Lemon operation detail mirrors the Mercado Pago receipt with full transparency, the user does not allege any misleading Lemon representation, and Lemon's own descargo points to published safety guidance on its official channels. In dubio pro consumidor is a tiebreaker; there is no genuine doubt to break here, because the user's evidence affirmatively establishes a voluntary push payment to a third-party scammer.

The proper remedy for the user is criminal and civil action against the named recipient (Braian Daniel Martinez, CUIT 20-38832689-2), with Lemon expected to cooperate with judicial information requests. That, however, is not what is before this panel. On the consumer-protection question actually presented — whether Lemon owes a refund under Sección A — the answer on this record is no. Vote: Favor Lemon, high confidence.
