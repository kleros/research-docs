# Case 89 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica"
**Promo / product at issue:** None — third-party "CEO" scam via WhatsApp impersonating Lemon; user voluntarily withdrew BTC from Lemon to an external wallet
**Amount in dispute:** ~170 Peruvian soles (deposits of 100 + 20 + 50 PEN), corresponding to the BTC withdrawal of 0.00016087 BTC shown in the screenshot
**Question:** Is Lemon liable to reimburse a user who voluntarily sent crypto, from her own Lemon wallet, to a third party on WhatsApp who falsely claimed to be a Lemon representative?

## Options
- Favor User / Favor Lemon / Refuse to Arbitrate

## Governing rules
1. **Protección al consumidor** — duty to act so the consumer's economic interests are safeguarded against foreseeable harms attributable to the provider.
2. **Información** — Lemon must inform users clearly about how to operate safely; the offer "as presented" governs.
3. **Transparencia** — no hidden restrictions; product must operate as described.
4. **Buena fe** — both parties must act in good faith.
5. **In dubio pro consumidor** — tiebreaker only when, after applying 1–4, the case remains balanced.
6. **Educación** — provider must educate the user about safe use, scams, and red flags.
7. **No discriminación** — n/a.
8. **T&Cs vs Section A** — T&Cs cannot override Section A duties, but Section A does not convert Lemon into a universal guarantor of off-platform fraud by unrelated third parties.

## Burden of proof
On the user, as claimant seeking reimbursement, to show Lemon (not a third party) caused or is contractually/legally responsible for the loss.

## Application
- **Protección:** The harm was caused by a third-party WhatsApp impersonator, not by any failure of Lemon's platform. The withdrawal itself executed correctly. No Section-A protection duty extends to indemnifying users for voluntary transfers to scammers reached outside official channels.
- **Información:** User does not allege Lemon misrepresented the product. The offer-as-presented (a self-custody-style wallet that executes user-initiated withdrawals) was delivered as described.
- **Transparencia:** No hidden term was applied against the user. The withdrawal screen showed destination wallet and status ("Completo").
- **Buena fe:** Lemon's response is measured and on-point, not evasive. The user acted in good faith but towards the scammer, not towards Lemon. Good faith on the user's side does not create a Lemon obligation where none existed.
- **In dubio:** Not engaged — the facts are clear that the counterparty was a WhatsApp third party labelled "CEO", openly told the user to send funds, and the user complied. No genuine tie.
- **Educación:** Lemon provides security content on its verified official Instagram and other channels, including a dedicated "Seguridad" highlight. Unrebutted. This satisfies the educational duty at the level reasonably demanded by Section A; it does not require preventing all off-platform social engineering.
- **No discriminación:** Not at issue.
- **T&C compatibility:** Lemon does not lean on a hidden carve-out; it argues a basic causation/scope point — third-party fraud outside the commercial relationship is not Lemon's liability. This is consistent with Section A: Section A regulates the provider's conduct toward the consumer, not third-party criminal acts against the consumer.

## Jurisdiction / Morality / Validity
Pass. Correct subcourt (consumer/blockchain-non-technical). No morality bar — the user is a scam victim, not a wrongdoer; Lemon is not accused of theft, only of denying reimbursement. Question is well-formed and answerable.

## Schelling-Point check
A panel of diligent Section-A-literate jurors will read the same screenshots, see a WhatsApp contact called "CEO" — clearly not Lemon — instruct the user to send funds, and see Lemon's platform execute a user-authorised withdrawal exactly as offered. The obvious, literal reading is that Lemon owes nothing here. Favor Lemon is the focal answer.

## Bias audit
The user's personal circumstances (small children, sick child, poverty, Peruvian soles) are emotionally powerful and the loss is small in absolute terms — there is pressure to "find a way" to award her something. That pressure must be set aside: Section A applies to provider conduct, and no clause converts Lemon into an insurer against off-platform impersonation scams when its own channels (verified Instagram, in-app withdrawal flow) functioned correctly and security information is published. Vote reflects the policy, not sympathy.

## VOTE
**Favor Lemon**

**Confidence:** high

## On-chain justification (2–3 short paragraphs, English)
Under Política Section A, Lemon's duties of Protección, Información, Transparencia, Buena Fe and Educación are owed in respect of the provider–consumer relationship. The user's own evidence — WhatsApp screenshots with a contact saved as "CEO" — shows unambiguously that the counterparty was a third party communicating outside any official Lemon channel, and that the user voluntarily executed a BTC withdrawal from her Lemon wallet to an external address belonging to that third party. The Lemon platform performed exactly as offered: the withdrawal completed and was confirmed in-app. There is no allegation, and no evidence, that Lemon misrepresented the product, applied a hidden term, denied access, or operated a faulty channel.

Lemon's unrebutted evidence further shows it maintains a verified official Instagram presence with a dedicated "Seguridad" highlight and publishes security guidance through official channels, satisfying the Educación duty to the standard Section A reasonably requires. Section A does not convert a wallet provider into a guarantor against social-engineering by unrelated impersonators reached off-platform; that risk falls on the user once she chose to follow a stranger's instructions on WhatsApp. In dubio pro consumidor is a tiebreaker and does not engage here, because the facts on causation and scope are not in genuine doubt.

The user's hardship is real and the loss matters to her, but sympathy cannot manufacture a Section-A breach where none is shown. Applying the policy literally and as a Schelling-point reading, the correct vote is **Favor Lemon**.
