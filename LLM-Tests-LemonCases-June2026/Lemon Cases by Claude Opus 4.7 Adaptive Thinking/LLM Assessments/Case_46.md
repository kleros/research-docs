# Case 46 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica"
**Promo / product at issue:** No promotion. Outbound Lemon peer-to-peer transfers (ARS) made by the user to third-party CBU/CVU accounts under the influence of a "task scam" (fake Mercado Libre "tareas / recargas / comisiones" scheme run via Telegram by "Gerente senior-018" / "Joaquín").
**Amount in dispute:** At least ARS 94,831.00 (08-Jul-2024, 16:07) + ARS 269,000.00 (08-Jul-2024, 17:10) = ARS 363,831.00 documented in the Lemon transfer receipts; the user also references further "recargas" and a fictitious 1,500,000-peso "withdrawal tax". Exact total claimed is not stated by the user.
**Question:** Must Lemon Cash reimburse a user who voluntarily executed outbound transfers from his Lemon account to third-party CBU/CVU accounts after being deceived off-platform (Telegram) by a "task scam" operator unrelated to Lemon?

## Options
- Favor User
- Favor Lemon
- Refuse to Arbitrate

## Governing rules
1. Política de Protección al Consumidor — Section A:
   1. Protección al usuario
   2. Información (clara y veraz sobre los productos y condiciones)
   3. Transparencia
   4. Buena fe
   5. In dubio pro consumidor (tiebreaker)
   6. Educación financiera
   7. No discriminación
2. Section B — T&Cs apply only insofar as compatible with Section A. T&Cs catch-alls cannot defeat a Section A violation.
3. Operational baseline for peer-to-peer fiat transfers: once a user authenticates and confirms a transfer to a CBU/CVU/alias chosen by the user, Lemon executes the user's instruction. Liability arises only if Lemon itself misrepresented something, failed a security/KYC duty, or refused a reasonable post-fraud assistance request.

## Burden of proof
The user, as claimant, must show (a) what Lemon did or failed to do that caused or contributed to the loss, and (b) that Lemon's act/omission breaches a Section A principle. The user must do more than show that he was scammed by a third party.

## Application
- **Protección:** Lemon's protective duty does not extend to second-guessing authenticated, user-confirmed outbound P2P transfers absent obvious systemic anomalies. Two transfers totalling ~ARS 364k inside one afternoon, with motivo "Varios", to two different individuals, are within the normal envelope of authorised user activity. No fact suggests Lemon failed to honour any legitimate post-fraud assistance request. No breach demonstrated.
- **Información:** The "offer as presented" doctrine bites on promotional / product claims by Lemon. Here Lemon made no offer to the user — the offer that misled the user came from "Gerente senior-018"/"Joaquín" on Telegram. There is no Lemon representation to test. No breach.
- **Transparencia:** Receipts produced by Lemon show full counter-party data (name, CUIT, CBU/CVU, motivo, transaction ID), i.e., Lemon was transparent about where the money went. The user himself supplied these receipts. No breach.
- **Buena fe:** Nothing on the record shows bad faith by Lemon. The descargo is brief but accurate to the evidence and the user does not allege any specific Lemon misconduct (e.g., support refusal, account freeze, chargeback failure). No breach.
- **In dubio pro consumidor:** Tiebreaker only. There is no genuine evidentiary doubt — the cause of loss is documented and is an off-platform third-party fraud induced by Telegram messages, with the user manually executing each transfer.
- **Educación financiera:** Lemon's official Instagram and in-app security content is offered as evidence of fulfilling its educational duty around common scam patterns ("task scam" is widely warned about). The user's own evidence shows red flags (unsolicited Telegram contact, "recargas" to a personal CBU, escalating "commission" promises, fictitious withdrawal tax). Not breached.
- **No discriminación:** Not at issue.
- **T&C compatibility:** Lemon's reliance on the user's voluntary instruction is consistent with both the T&Cs and Section A. T&Cs are not being used to immunise a Section A breach because no Section A breach is shown.

## Jurisdiction / Morality / Validity
The case is within the subcourt's scope (Lemon consumer-protection complaint, peer fiat transfer). Claim is intelligible and the parties' positions are properly framed. No basis for Refuse to Arbitrate.

## Schelling-Point check
A diligent Spanish-speaking juror, looking at receipts to unrelated CBU/CVU accounts, a Telegram "Gerente senior-018" scam conversation, no Lemon support chat, and no specific Lemon misconduct allegation, will conclude that Lemon is not the cause of the loss. The convergent answer is Favor Lemon.

## Bias audit
Sympathy for a victim of a cruel "task scam" is appropriate but cannot transfer liability to a payment intermediary that merely executed authenticated instructions. Reasoning above does not rely on victim-blaming; it relies on the absence of any pleaded or evidenced Lemon act or omission that violates Section A.

## VOTE
**Favor Lemon**

**Confidence:** high

## On-chain justification (2–3 short paragraphs, English)

The user's own evidence — a 69-page chat with a Telegram contact called "Gerente senior-018" / "Joaquín" running a fake Mercado Libre "tareas / recargas / comisiones" scheme, plus two Lemon transfer receipts (ARS 94,831 at 16:07 and ARS 269,000 at 17:10 on 08-Jul-2024) to two unrelated CBU/CVU accounts — establishes only that the user was defrauded off-platform by a third party and then voluntarily executed authenticated outbound transfers through Lemon to that third party's accounts. There is no Lemon offer at issue, no Lemon support-chat assurance, and no specific allegation of any Lemon act or omission.

Applying the Política de Protección al Consumidor, no Section A principle is breached. Información and Transparencia concern Lemon's own representations; here Lemon's receipts disclose counter-party identity, CBU/CVU, motivo, and transaction ID. Protección does not require Lemon to override the user's own confirmed P2P instructions to arbitrary CBU/CVU recipients; the operational pattern (two transfers, "Varios" motivo, sub-7-figure ARS amounts, different recipients) carries no objective fraud signal that Lemon should have blocked. Buena fe and No discriminación are not engaged. Educación is supported by Lemon's Instagram security content. In dubio pro consumidor is a tiebreaker and the record contains no genuine doubt as to causation.

The loss originated in a third-party off-platform fraud induced by the scammer's messages, and the user — not Lemon — chose the recipients and authorised each transfer. Section B T&C compatibility is not strained because Section A itself is not breached. The just outcome under the Política is to deny reimbursement against Lemon.
