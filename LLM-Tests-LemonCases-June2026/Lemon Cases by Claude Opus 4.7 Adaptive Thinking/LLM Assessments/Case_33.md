# Case 33 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica"
**Promo / product at issue:** P2P / off-platform transfer to a third-party "vendedor" (seller) on 18 May
**Amount in dispute:** Not stated (user requests at minimum a partial refund)
**Question:** Is Lemon liable to refund the user for a transfer the user voluntarily instructed to a third-party seller who allegedly failed to deliver goods?

## Options
- Favor User
- Favor Lemon
- Refuse to Arbitrate

## Governing rules
1. Protección al usuario (Section A.1)
2. Información (A.2)
3. Transparencia (A.3)
4. Buena fe (A.4)
5. In dubio pro consumidor — tiebreaker only (A.5)
6. Educación financiera (A.6)
7. No discriminación (A.7)
8. Section B: T&Cs cannot override Section A; but Section A is not a strict-liability rule.

## Burden of proof
User bears the initial burden to show Lemon (not the third-party seller) breached a duty. The user submitted no evidence at all.

## Application
- **Protección:** Protección protects users against acts/omissions by Lemon. The harm here was caused by an unrelated third-party seller off-platform. No Lemon act or omission is identified.
- **Información:** No information failure alleged or shown — user knew he was transferring funds to a third party and did so deliberately.
- **Transparencia:** No hidden term or opaque condition is in play. Transfer was instructed by the user with full awareness.
- **Buena fe:** Lemon acted in good faith by executing the user's own instruction; the user does not dispute the instruction. No bad-faith conduct by Lemon shown.
- **In dubio:** Tiebreaker only. There is no factual tie — facts clearly point to a P2P/commercial dispute between user and a third party.
- **Educación:** Lemon evidences active publication of security guidance (Wiki + Instagram). Compliant.
- **No discriminación:** Not in issue.
- **T&C compatibility:** No Section A principle is implicated such that any T&C would need to yield. Lemon's position is consistent with Section A.

## Jurisdiction / Morality / Validity
Dispute is intelligible, in scope, and not immoral or invalid. No basis for Refuse to Arbitrate.

## Schelling-Point check
A diligent, policy-literate juror would observe: (a) user voluntarily transferred to a third party, (b) user submitted zero evidence, (c) user himself admits the conflict is with the seller. Custodial wallet operators are not guarantors of off-platform commercial deals. The convergent answer is Favor Lemon.

## Bias audit
Sympathy for an "emprendedor" who lost money to a scam is not a legal basis. I have set that aside. I have also not penalised the user for lack of formal counsel — but I cannot manufacture evidence or a duty that was not pleaded.

## VOTE
**Favor Lemon**

**Confidence:** high

## On-chain justification (2–3 short paragraphs, English)

The user's own descargo admits this was a voluntary transfer on 18 May to a third-party "vendedor" who allegedly did not deliver goods. The user offered no evidence of any kind — no screenshots, no chats, no transaction record, no support-ticket reliance, no specific Lemon promise breached. Under Section A, Protección, Información, Transparencia and Buena fe protect users against Lemon's acts and omissions; they do not convert Lemon into a guarantor of every off-platform commercial deal a user chooses to enter. Nothing in the record identifies a Lemon act or omission that caused the loss.

Lemon, for its part, demonstrated that it acted only on the user's express instruction (uncontested by the user) and that it actively publishes security guidance via its official Wiki and verified Instagram, satisfying the Educación principle. The "in dubio pro consumidor" rule is a tiebreaker; here the facts are not in equipoise — the user himself frames the conflict as one with the seller, not with Lemon.

Accordingly, applying Section A faithfully and giving the user every reasonable benefit, the claim against Lemon fails. Vote: Favor Lemon, with high confidence.
