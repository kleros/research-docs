# Case 9 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica"
**Promo / product at issue:** "Reintegro Pagos de Servicios" — Oct 2023 promotion offering 50% reintegro on first 3 utility-bill payments made via Lemon's "Pagos de Servicios" function, plus a separate 1000 USDC sorteo. T&Cs filed are the official "Reintegro Pagos de Servicios" / "Sorteo Pagos de Servicios" Oct 2023 terms.
**Amount in dispute:** User claims 180 USD; Lemon disputes the figure as not matching actual payments. Promo cap per T&C and per banner is AR$1,000 per bill, max 3 bills (i.e. AR$3,000 maximum, far below 180 USD).
**Question:** Did Lemon's in-app banner advertising a "50% reintegro" for paying utility bills "con crypto o pesos" mislead the consumer into believing peso payments qualified, when the reintegro was in fact restricted to USDC payments?

## Options
- Favor User
- Favor Lemon
- Refuse to Arbitrate

## Governing rules
1. **Principle 1 — Protección al consumidor.**
2. **Principle 2 — Información clara, veraz y completa** (offer as presented).
3. **Principle 3 — Transparencia** — no publicidad engañosa; key conditions must appear in the principal advertising, not be relegated to letra chica.
4. **Principle 4 — Buena fe.**
5. **Principle 5 — In dubio pro consumidor** (tiebreaker only).
6. **TyC "Reintegro Pagos de Servicios" §1(v):** participant must use "Pagos de Servicios" AND pay with $USDC.
7. **TyC §2:** "Tope hasta el equivalente a 1000 ARS pesos por factura y hasta tres facturas por usuario."
8. **TyC §3:** "Las decisiones de la EMPRESA serán definitivas e irrecurribles" — catch-all immunity clause; under Section B this cannot override Section A.

## Burden of proof
User must show the advertised offer was misleading and that he reasonably relied on it. Lemon must show the controlling condition was clearly disclosed in the offer as presented to the consumer (in-app banner), not buried in linked T&Cs.

## Application
- **Protección:** Consumer is protected against misleading advertising. Here the consumer is protected, but the banner itself states the USDC condition expressly ("...el 50% de las 3 primeras facturas que pagues con USDC..."), so protection does not require ignoring a stated condition.
- **Información:** The banner is internally tense — it announces that the user "podés pagar todas tus facturas con crypto o pesos" and immediately afterwards limits the reintegro to facturas "que pagues con USDC". The dual statement is awkward, but the qualifying clause is on the face of the offer in the same sentence and same screen — not relegated to linked T&Cs. A reasonable reader of the entire banner (which is only six short lines) sees the USDC restriction. Información obligation is met, though imperfectly.
- **Transparencia:** Borderline. The "crypto o pesos" phrase explains which payment rails the "Pagos de Servicios" feature supports; the USDC clause explains which of those rails earns the reintegro. The agent's concession that the team will "hacer el mensaje más claro la próxima vez" admits the wording is suboptimal, but the controlling condition is nonetheless present in the principal advert. This is not a "buried in T&Cs" case.
- **Buena fe:** No bad-faith conduct on Lemon's side evidenced — the condition was stated up front, support engaged the user, T&Cs are coherent with the banner. User also acted in good faith based on his reading.
- **In dubio:** Not invoked. There is no true equipoise — the qualifying clause appears in the banner itself.
- **Educación / No discriminación:** N/A.
- **T&C compatibility (Section B):** The TyC's USDC requirement and AR$1,000 per-bill / 3-bill cap are also stated on the face of the banner, so they are compatible with Principles 2 and 3. TyC §3's catch-all ("decisiones definitivas e irrecurribles") is NOT compatible with Section A and cannot bar this arbitration — but Lemon does not need it to win here, and the panel does not rely on it.

## Jurisdiction / Morality / Validity
Properly framed consumer-protection dispute, within the Blockchain No Técnica subcourt's scope. No RtA grounds.

## Schelling-Point check
A diligent juror who reads the banner image will see the USDC condition stated in the same six-line block. The "crypto o pesos" phrase is awkward but does not negate the immediately-following USDC qualifier. The honest answer is that the controlling condition was disclosed in the principal advertising — not in T&Cs only. Most jurors will land on Favor Lemon. The agent's "lo tendremos en cuenta" remark is a courtesy admission of imperfect wording, not an admission that the condition was hidden.

## Bias audit
There is a natural pull toward the consumer in consumer-protection cases. I have checked that pull against the actual banner text. The banner is not a "buried letra chica" case (compare cases where USDC requirement only appears in linked T&Cs). The user's claimed 180 USD figure is also incompatible with the per-bill cap stated on the banner face, which weighs against generous quantum framing.

## VOTE
**Favor Lemon**

**Confidence:** medium

## On-chain justification (2–3 short paragraphs, English)

Under Principle 2 (Información) and Principle 3 (Transparencia), the controlling condition of the promotion — that the 50% reintegro applies only to bills paid with USDC — was stated on the face of the in-app banner itself ("el 50% de las 3 primeras facturas que pagues con USDC hasta el 13/10"), not relegated to linked T&Cs. The banner's earlier reference to "crypto o pesos" describes which payment rails the "Pagos de Servicios" feature supports; the USDC clause in the same sentence explains which of those rails earns the reintegro. The Section B compatibility check on the TyC §1(v) USDC requirement is therefore satisfied — the condition is consistent with the offer as presented to the consumer.

The user's reliance argument is weakened by the fact that the limit he says was hidden is not, in fact, hidden — it sits in the very paragraph he screenshotted. The support agent's polite concession that the team will "hacer el mensaje más claro la próxima vez" is an acknowledgement of imperfect wording, not an admission that the condition was concealed. Principle 4 (Buena fe) is satisfied on both sides. Principle 5 (In dubio pro consumidor) is not engaged because the banner is not in true equipoise — a careful reading of the six-line text yields the USDC qualifier.

Quantum is independently fatal to the user's 180 USD figure. The banner itself caps the benefit at AR$1,000 per factura and three facturas — a maximum of AR$3,000, far below the claimed amount. TyC §3's "decisiones definitivas e irrecurribles" clause is incompatible with Section A and is disregarded under Section B, but Lemon does not need it: the merits favour Lemon on the disclosure question.
