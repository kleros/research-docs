# Case 6 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica"
**Promo / product at issue:** Delisting / forced conversion of LUNC (Luna Classic) and USTC holdings into USDC / ARS by Lemon Cash as of 1 June
**Amount in dispute:** ~USD 1–2 (15,000 LUNC tokens converted to ~1 USDC); user invokes hypothetical future value
**Question:** Was Lemon required to either send the user's LUNC to an external wallet or hold it in custody rather than auto-converting it to USDC at market value upon delisting?

## Options
- Favor User
- Favor Lemon
- Refuse to Arbitrate

## Governing rules
1. Section A.2 (Información) and A.3 (Transparencia): Lemon must give clear, truthful, complete information about price, characteristics and contracting conditions, including delisting mechanics.
2. Section A.4 (Buena fe): both parties must act honestly and fairly; user's expectation must be reasonable.
3. Section B compatibility test: T&C clause 3.1 (right to delist) is only enforceable if it is compatible with Section A principles and was communicated transparently.

## Burden of proof
User must show Lemon's delisting/conversion procedure was misleading, inadequately disclosed, or denied a feasible alternative; once advance notice with reasons is shown, the burden shifts back.

|---|---|---|---|---|

- User's hypothetical future appreciation of LUNC to USD 1 (not a vested right, not a present loss).
- User's proposed remedy of an in-app "safe-box" feature (not contractually owed).

## Application (principle-by-principle)
- **Protección:** User is the vulnerable party, but protection covers reasonable expectations grounded in disclosed terms — not speculative future gains on a delisted token.
- **Información:** Lemon sent a clear advance email stating (a) the delisting date, (b) the conversion mechanic for each token, and (c) the specific reason no external withdrawal option was offered (balance below minimum network fee). Information was complete and truthful.
- **Transparencia:** No misleading practice identified. The email explicitly addresses the exact issue the user raises (why no withdrawal option). Conversion price reflected market value at the time (~USD 1–2 → ~1 USDC).
- **Buena fe:** Lemon acted in good faith — gave notice, explained reasoning, converted to a stable equivalent rather than zeroing the balance. User's request to be paid on hypothetical future value is not a good-faith claim.
- **In dubio pro consumidor:** Not invoked — no genuine ambiguity; Lemon's notice is unambiguous.
- **Educación / No discriminación:** n/a.
- **T&C compatibility (Section B):** Clause 3.1 (right to delist) is compatible with Section A because Lemon paired it with advance notice, a stated reason, and a reasonable conversion mechanism. The clause is not used as a catch-all immunity here; it is exercised transparently.

## Jurisdiction / Morality / Validity check
Proper subcourt; consumer-protection dispute properly framed; no morality trigger; materials sufficient.

## Schelling-Point check
A diligent, policy-literate juror would conclude Lemon gave proper notice, explained the technical impossibility of withdrawing dust below the network fee, and converted at fair market value. The user's claim rests on speculative future appreciation, which is not a protectable interest.

## Bias audit
No pro-platform bias: outcome would be different if Lemon had given no notice, converted below market, or hidden the fee rationale. No anti-user bias: user is simply not legally entitled to retain a delisted token indefinitely or be compensated at hypothetical future prices.

## VOTE
**Favor Lemon**

**Confidence:** high

## On-chain justification (2–3 short paragraphs, English)

Under Principle A.2 (Información) and A.3 (Transparencia), Lemon's own email — submitted by the user as primary evidence — disclosed the delisting date, the auto-conversion mechanism for both LUNC and USTC, and the specific technical reason no external-wallet withdrawal was offered (the holding was below the network withdrawal fee). The information presented to the consumer was clear, advance, and complete; there is no misleading practice.

Under Principle A.4 (Buena fe), converting a sub-fee dust balance to a stable equivalent at prevailing market value is a reasonable, good-faith resolution. The user's claim — that Lemon should compensate for a speculative future recovery of LUNC to USD 1 — is not a present right and is not protected by any Section A principle. Under Section B, T&C clause 3.1 (delisting right) is compatible with Section A in this instance because it was exercised with notice, reasons, and a fair conversion rather than as catch-all immunity. In dubio pro consumidor is not triggered: there is no material ambiguity.

For these reasons the juror votes **Favor Lemon**.
