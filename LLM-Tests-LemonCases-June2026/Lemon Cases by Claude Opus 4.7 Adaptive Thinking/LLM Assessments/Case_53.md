# Case 53 — Kleros Juror Assessment

**Court:** Kleros "Blockchain No Técnica" (Lemon Cash consumer arbitration)
**Promo / product at issue:** "Pagá servicios en Lemon" — 10% cashback in USDT on bill payments via the Lemon app, cap ARS 2,000, valid until 24/07/2024.
**Amount in dispute:** Up to ARS 2,000 cashback (10% of ARS 20,250 = ARS 2,025, capped at ARS 2,000).
**Question:** Is Lemon required to honour the 10% USDT cashback for a user who paid an ARS 20,250 Claro bill through the Lemon app on 18/07/2024 within the promotion period?

## Options
- **Favor User** — Lemon must pay the cashback (up to the ARS 2,000 cap).
- **Favor Lemon** — Cashback denied (e.g. eligibility/method restriction not met).
- **Refuse to Arbitrate** — Question malformed/out of jurisdiction/morality issue.

## Governing rules
1. **Protección al consumidor** — interpret promo terms in favour of the consumer where ambiguous.
2. **Información** — promo must clearly state material conditions, restrictions, and exclusions.
3. **Transparencia** — the offer "as presented" governs. Hidden eligibility limits cannot be retroactively imposed.
4. **Buena fe** — Lemon must act in good faith and honour commitments made to users.
5. **In dubio pro consumidor** — tiebreaker when interpretation is genuinely ambiguous.
6. **Educación financiera** — clarity of communication regarding crypto/financial mechanics.
7. **No discriminación** — equal treatment of eligible users.
8. **Section B** — T&Cs cannot override Section A principles; promo wording binds Lemon.

## Burden of proof
On Lemon (the offeror) to prove the user did not meet stated eligibility, OR to prove the offer "as presented" excluded this transaction. Default = honour the promotion as worded.

## Application
- **Protección:** User is a consumer paying a regulated utility bill through Lemon's promoted channel; full protection applies.
- **Información:** The promotional email defines the eligible action as paying a bill "en Lemon" via "Inicio → Pagar mis servicios" — exactly what the user did. No "card-only" restriction appears anywhere in the offer.
- **Transparencia:** The offer "as presented" explicitly states "Podés pagar con cualquier moneda, pesos o crypto" — confirming the broadest payment-method scope. Any narrower internal restriction was hidden and cannot bind the user.
- **Buena fe:** Lemon expressly admits (descargo, p.1) that a database error denied cashback to users "que cumplían con los requisitos" — i.e. eligible users were wrongly excluded. Good faith requires remediation.
- **In dubio:** Not needed — there is no genuine ambiguity once Lemon's own admission is read.
- **Educación:** Not engaged.
- **No discriminación:** Lemon's own statement implies an arbitrary technical exclusion of some eligible users — a discrimination/equal-treatment problem the principle directly addresses.
- **T&C compatibility:** No T&C clause is invoked that could override Section A; even a catch-all could not immunise Lemon from its own admitted failure to deliver the promised benefit to eligible users.

## Jurisdiction / Morality / Validity
Pass — consumer promo dispute, properly in "Blockchain No Técnica"; no morality issue; question is well-formed and answerable.

## Schelling-Point check
Strong. With Lemon's explicit written admission that its system failed eligible users, a panel of diligent jurors converges on "Favor User". No contrarian reading survives the admission.

## Bias audit
Decision rests on (i) the offer text submitted by both parties and (ii) Lemon's own admission. No reliance on sympathy, rhetoric, or party identity.

## VOTE
**Favor User**

**Confidence:** high

## On-chain justification (English)
The promotional email submitted by both parties offered 10% USDT cashback (capped at ARS 2,000) for any utility bill paid through the Lemon app between launch and 24/07/2024, with the express clarification that payment could be made "con cualquier moneda, pesos o crypto" via the in-app flow "Inicio → Pagar mis servicios". The user paid an ARS 20,250 Claro bill via that exact flow on 18/07/2024, well within the promotional window. Under Section A principles of Información and Transparencia, the offer "as presented" governs — any "card-only" restriction asserted later by support is not in the published promo and therefore cannot bind the user.

Lemon's own descargo is dispositive. It restates the promo terms identically to the user's evidence and then expressly admits: "Desde Lemon detectamos un error en nuestra base de datos que impidió que algunos usuarios que cumplían con los requisitos recibieran el reintegro de la promoción". This is an admission against interest that the user met the requirements and that Lemon's system wrongly withheld the benefit. The principles of Buena fe, Protección al consumidor, and No discriminación all require Lemon to honour the cashback to affected eligible users; Section B prevents any T&C catch-all from overriding these duties.

Vote: **Favor User** — Lemon must credit the 10% USDT cashback up to the ARS 2,000 cap on the ARS 20,250 Claro payment of 18/07/2024.
